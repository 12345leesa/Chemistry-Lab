P Block


Location in the Periodic Table

  • Groups 13-18: P-block elements include boron, carbon, nitrogen, oxygen, halogens, and noble gases.
  • Their outermost electrons are in the p-orbital (e.g., Group 13: ns² np¹, Group 14: ns² np²).
  • Includes **metals, metalloids, and nonmetals**, making it the most diverse block.

Key Characteristics

  • Group 13: Ends with ns² np¹ (e.g., Boron: 1s² 2s² 2p¹).
  • Group 14: Ends with ns² np² (e.g., Carbon: 1s² 2s² 2p²).
  • Group 15: Ends with ns² np³ (e.g., Nitrogen: 1s² 2s² 2p³).
  • Group 16: Ends with ns² np⁴ (e.g., Oxygen: 1s² 2s² 2p⁴).
  • Group 17: Ends with ns² np⁵ (e.g., Fluorine: 1s² 2s² 2p⁵).
  • Group 18: Ends with ns² np⁶ (e.g., Neon: 1s² 2s² 2p⁶).
  • Reactivity:
    • Decreases down the group for nonmetals (e.g., fluorine is more reactive than iodine).
    • Increases down the group for metals (e.g., thallium is more reactive than boron).
  • Oxidation States: Vary across groups (e.g., Group 14: +4 or -4, Group 17: -1).
  • Physical Properties: Includes gases (oxygen, nitrogen), liquids (bromine), and solids (carbon, phosphorus).

Trends in the Groups

  • Atomic Radius: Increases down a group.
  • Ionization Energy: Decreases down a group.
  • Electronegativity: Decreases down the group for nonmetals.
  • Melting/Boiling Points: Vary significantly (e.g., noble gases have low boiling points).
