Safety Guidelines


Wear PPE

Wearing appropriate PPE, such as gloves, goggles, and lab coats, is a fundamental aspect of safety in hazardous environments. It protects workers from a variety of risks, ensuring their health and safety while promoting a culture of safety in the workplace. Always assess the specific risks associated with the job and select the appropriate PPE accordingly.

Maintain Cleanliness

Maintaining clean and organized work areas is essential for safety, efficiency, and overall workplace morale. By implementing best practices and fostering a culture of cleanliness, organizations can significantly reduce the risk of accidents and spills while promoting a productive and positive environment for all employees.

Know Emergency Equipment

Knowing the location and proper use of safety equipment like fire extinguishers, eye wash stations, and emergency showers is critical in preventing and responding to emergencies. By fostering a culture of safety and ensuring that employees are well-informed and trained, organizations can significantly enhance workplace safety and reduce the risk of injury or damage.

Handle Pipettes with Care

Use pipette bulbs or pumps instead of mouth pipetting to avoid exposure to harmful chemicals. Clean pipettes properly after use and store them in designated holders.
